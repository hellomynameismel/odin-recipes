Hole in One

Home

Fried egg cooked in the middle of a toast

When you wake up hungry and feel playful, a hole in one hits just the spot.

Put on some music, grab your favorite bread (I'm a huge fan cinnamon-raisin bread for this) and don't be shy with the butter.

Let's go!

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of cinnamon-raisin bread (or use whatever you'd like)
  • 2 large eggs
  • pat of butter
  • salt and pepper
  • maple syrup (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat a cast-iron skillet to medium high.
  2. Cut out a hole in your toast. Feel free to use a knife (neater) or just tear with your hands (more fun).
  3. Butter the pan lightly, then place the bread down (wiping the butter on it).
  4. After a few mins (when bread side down is toasted), butter the top of the bread then flip over.
  5. Crack the egg in a bowl and season with S&P. Slide an egg in each hole.
  6. When the egg white starts to turn opaque at the bottom, add a pat of butter to the top of the egg, then flip again. The butter is optional if your pan is well-seasoned, but it definitely adds flavor!
  7. Flip onto your plate when the eggs are done. Timing depends on how runny you want the yolks. I take mine off after about twenty seconds and top is just solidified, with the whites slightly translucent and the yolk runny.

Et voila! Congrats on your hole in one!
